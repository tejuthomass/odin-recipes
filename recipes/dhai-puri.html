Dhai Puri

Description

Craving for a plate full of Dahi Puri? So, why go out when you can make this quick and easy Dahi Puri recipe at home?

This amazing street food has many names, such as Dahi Wale Gol Gappe, Dahi Puri, or Dahi Patasha. This is a delicacy that will make your mouth water in an instant. Those who love chaat cannot resist this popular street food available across North India. Made with crispy puris, boiled potatoes, onions, tomatoes, yoghurt and sev, this easy-to-make snack recipe is loved by people of all age groups. You can buy the pani puris from the market or you can also prepare them at home as well. These puris are stuffed with spicy potato filling and then topped with sweet and chilled curd along with chutneys. You can use the tamarind chutney as well, but it would be slightly on the sweet side. The crunchiness of the puris paired with the tangy curd is a heavenly treat for your taste buds. This chaat recipe can easily be prepared at home in a jiffy. It can be served as an after-school snack for the kids. It can also be served at kitty parties, anniversaries, and potlucks. Serve them fresh because they get soggy after some time due to the curd mixture and green/red chutney. Or you can pour in the curd and the chutney when you are about to serve it. So, what are you waiting for? Try this recipe now and earn compliments from your loved ones.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 potato
  • 3/4 green chillies
  • 2 and 1/2 teaspoon green chutney
  • 1/4 handful coriander leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/5 medium onion
  • 3 and 1/4 tablespoon yoghurt (curd)
  • 1 teaspoon pani puris
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chilli powder
  • 1 and 1/2 tablespoon sev
  • 1/4 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/4 teaspoon cumin powder
  • a quarter-sized tomato
  • salt as required

Steps

  1. To prepare this delicious chaat recipe, wash the potatoes and add them to a pressure cooker with enough water and close the lid. Put it over medium heat and boil the potatoes for 2-3 whistles. Once done, take them out, peel and mash them.
  2. To make the potato filling, in the bowl of mashed potatoes, add red chilli powder, cumin powder and chaat masala. Mix all the ingredients together.
  3. Take another bowl and beat the curd with salt and sugar properly. Next, finely chop the onion, tomato and green chillies.
  4. Now, the time has come for plating. On a large plate, arrange all the puris and make a hole in the centre of each of them. Fill them with potato filling and chopped veggies. Top them up with the beaten curd mixture and lastly, garnish with the green chutney and sev on top. Enjoy fresh Dahi Puri Chaat.
