Dosa

Description

Talk about South Indian cuisine and the one thing that will surely come to your mind is dosa or dosai! A dosai is a popular and classic dish of South Indian cuisine and is a versatile dish that can be prepared anytime and on any occasion. This dosai recipe is plain and made without any filling and is enjoyed best with coconut chutney and sambar. And you will be surprised to know that making dosa is not that difficult. It is prepared with ingredients that are easily available in your kitchen, like rice, urad dal, basmati rice, fenugreek seeds or methi dana, and groundnut oil. This is an easy-to-make and a must-try dish when guests are coming over in a short time period, as you can store the unfermented dosa batter for a day or two. Do not wait much longer, and make this dosa recipe today at home for your family and friends, and enjoy it with your loved ones!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup and 3 teaspoons of rice
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 2 tablespoons of groundnut oil
  • 3 tablespoons of urad dal
  • salt as required
  • 2 tablespoon of basmati rice

Steps

  1. To prepare this delicious plain dosa recipe, first you need to make the dosa batter. For that, wash rice and urad dal in a bowl together under running water and then drain the extra water. Soak them both in fresh water along with fenugreek seeds overnight or for 6–8 hours. The next day, drain the extra water and add the soaked dal along with methi dana in a blender jar along with salt and water (1/4 cup). Reserve it for at least 5 hours after grinding to a fine paste. Once done, divide it into 8 equal portions and keep it aside. The dosa batter is now ready. 
  2. Next, peel an onion and cut it into halves. Tie one half of the cut onions in a muslin cloth and dip it in cooking oil. Rub the surface of a pre-heated griddle with the flat side. Warm the griddle over low heat and spread a portion of the batter over the surface by moving a ladle in concentric circles from the middle outwards. 
  3. When little bubbles appear on the rapidly cooking pancake, flip it over and cook for a few seconds. Ensure that the pancake does not become crisp. Repeat the process with the remaining portions of batter and make more such dosas.
