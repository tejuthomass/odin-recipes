Sambar Sadam

Description

Call it sambar rice or Sambar Sadam in the Tamil language, this main dish is a famous South Indian recipe that you can quickly prepare at home for your loved ones! Looking similar to bisi bele bhath, it is truly delicious and has a taste that you can relish with your loved ones on occasions like game night and simple lunch. This vegetarian recipe is cooked using toor dal, rice, grated coconut, onion, carrot, tamarind, beans, potatoes, okra and a melange of authentic spices of southern India. Cooked under 15 minutes, this is a quick recipe that you easily prepare and we can bet, your guests will be licking their fingers. With a tangy yet spicy flavour in this dish, you do not need to make anything else as it will give your taste buds an experience that everyone will relish. So, don't wait much and try this easy recipe at home and enjoy it with your loved ones!

Ingredients

  • 1/4 stalks curry leaves
  • 1/4 tablespoon asafoetida
  • 1/4 carrot
  • salt As required
  • 1/4 tomato
  • 1/3 medium onion
  • a half teaspoon chana dal
  • 1/4 teaspoon of mustard seeds
  • 1 and 1/4 tablespoon bean
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 okra
  • water as required
  • 2 and 3/4 tablespoon grated coconut
  • 1/2 teaspoon ghee
  • a quarter teaspoon turmeric
  • tamarind, as needed
  • 1/3 teaspoon urad dal
  • 1/4 red chilli
  • 1/3 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1/4 potato
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1/3 tablespoon refined oil

Steps

  1. To prepare this main dish recipe, take a chopping board and chop all the vegetables. Next, wash and soak the toor dal in a large bowl. Once done, put a pressure cooker on medium flame and add toor dal in it, cook for 3 whistles. Once cooked, mash and keep the dal aside. Then, soak the tamarind in water and extract its juice.
  2. Now, for preparing the sambar sadam masala , heat oil in a pan and add chana dal, coriander seeds, black pepper, red chilli. Saute until they become golden brown. Then add this mixture to the grinder along with grated coconut and water. Grind them to make a fine paste. Pressure cook the rice and keep it aside.
  3. Next, take a pan over medium flame and heat oil in it. Once it is heated add mustard seeds, asafoetida, red chilli, fenugreek seeds and curry leaves. When it starts spluttering add chopped onions and saute until it becomes translucent. Once done, add all the vegetables and fry them well. Add the required amount of water and cook for 2 minutes. When the vegetables are half cooked, add turmeric powder, salt, tamarind juice and cook again for 3 minutes.
  4. Then, add mashed toor dal, ground masala and mix them well with the vegetable mixture. Add cooked rice along with 1 cup of water and simmer for 5 minutes.
  5. Finally, add ghee and turn off the burner. Transfer the sambar sadam to a serving dish and garnish it with chopped coriander leaves. Enjoy!
